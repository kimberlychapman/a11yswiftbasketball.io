Just like how there are many different ways to go about scoring (Offense), there are many different ways to go about preventing the other team from scoring (Defense).

Two common Defensive Strategies are:

1. Person-to-Person Defense

2. Zone Defense
Person-to-Person is the most common Defense. In Person-to-Person Defense, each player is matched with a player on the opposing team— and they guard that person the entire time.

A team will play Person-to-Person if:
● Each player on your team matches up with a player on the opposing team.

● In other words, their
skills are equal .

● OR the other team has a really strong offensive player.
X

In a Zone Defense, each player guards a specific area; they guard anyone who comes into their space: making sure that no one who enters their space scores.

A team will play a Zone if:

● There are players on the other team that are not strong shooters (so if you can keep them from getting close to the basket, they will have a much harder time scoring).

● OR there is a player (or two) on your team that is not a strong defender (so a zone allows a strong defender in the area next to theirs to help them).
