You have written offensive plays for when players should pass, dribble, or take a shot. But what does a team do when none of these three things are possible? One way that teams solve this problem is by Setting a Screen.

Setting a Screen is when an offensive player stands in the way of a defender who is guarding their teammate. By standing there, they block the defender from guarding their teammate— freeing up their teammate to pass or score. There are 4 steps to Setting a Screen:   signalPlayer( ), sprintToPlayer( ), standByDefender( ), stayStill( ).
Step 1 signaPlayerl( ) : Signal your teammate that you are going to set a screen for them; signal quickly so that the opposing team doesn't have time to stop you.
Step 2 sprintToPlayer( ): Sprint to your teammate to set the screen.

Step 3 standByDefender( ): Stop and stand beside or behind defender to block them from guarding your teammate.
Step 4 stayStill( ): Set your feet and use your arms to protect your body (remember: your feet must stay planted, and you can't use your arms to block the defender). Once you've made contact with the defender you can move again.
